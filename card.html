Dolphins

To prevent drowning while sleeping, only half of the dolphin’s brain goes to sleep while the other half remains awake so they can continue to breathe!

  • Scientific Name: Tursiops
  • Average Length: Up to 10 feet
  • Average Lifespan: Up to 60yrs
  • Habitat: Shallow areas of tropical and temperate oceans world-wide. Five species live in rivers world-wide.

Dolphins are well-documented as intelligent animals. Marine Scientists and Zoologists report that a dolphin’s large brain is structured for awareness and emotion. Dolphin brains are more structurally complex than humans. The dolphin brain comes very close to human brain aptitude levels.
